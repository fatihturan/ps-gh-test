Overview
Welcome to Pine Sports! We're dedicated to giving you cutting-edge easy-to-use analytics and AI tools, and letting you share your insights with the world.

Pine has three main sections: (1) Stories; (2) Explore; and (3) Predict. "Stories" aggregates all of the amazing sports insights that you generate, and provides the Pine Sports community with a forum to discuss and debate these insights. "Explore" allows you to analyze sports data with the same cutting-edge technology used by the biggest New York hedge funds and Silicon Valley tech companies. And "Predict" lets you build your own artificial intelligence models to predicting winners, winners against the spread and over-unders.

Stories
Stories is where you can post your thoughts and share your insights. Importantly, when building a Story, you can share and discuss the data visualizations you build with the tools available to you in Explore.

Explore
Explore gives you access to years of historic sports data, and lets you analyze the data in ways youâ€™ve never been able to do before. Explore has four sections:

  1. View is a simple spreadsheet view of the data. Here, you can sort and filter the data any way you like.

  2. Visualize is a tool that allows you to build powerful data visualizations to share with the Pine community.

  3. Correlate gives you the tools to better understand the relationships between data columns.

  4. Select allows you to select the stats you would like to view in the other three sections. It also gives you an overview of the data contained in each column.

Predict
Predict gives you access to cutting edge artificial intelligence so you can build your own models to predict the outcome of games. All you need to do is tell us (1) how many seasons you want to go back to teach your model; (2) how many games back you would like to look when comparing teams; and (3) the stats you think are important to make a prediction. We then run a bunch of AI models and pick the best one for your choices. Your "model score" is the percentage of games it got right when we tested the model without telling it the result (usually using the last season as a test).
